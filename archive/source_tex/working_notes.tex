\section{The Swiss-cheese direction}
\label{sec:sc-direction}
% =================================================================

The bar complex of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a curve~$X$ is a
cosheaf on $\Ran(X)$.  Its differential encodes collisions in the
$\C$-direction (the holomorphic plane).  Its coproduct encodes
separation in the $\R$-direction (the topological line).  Together:
a Swiss-cheese algebra on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \mathrm{Conf}_k(\R)$.

At genus~$0$, the Swiss-cheese structure is uncurved: the
$m_k^{\mathrm{SC}}$ operations satisfy the $\SCchtop$ axioms
without obstruction.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, curvature appears:
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$, where $\kappa$ is
the modular characteristic from Volume~I and $\omega_g$ is the
K\"ahler form on~$\cM_g$.

The recognition theorem (Weiss cosheaf descent) identifies
$\SCchtop$-algebras with factorization algebras on
$\C \times \R$ satisfying a local constancy condition along~$\R$.
The proof uses the product Weiss topology: a cover of
$U \times I \subset \C \times \R$ is a collection of products
$\{U_\alpha \times I_\beta\}$ refining the product open cover.

\section{PVA descent}
\label{sec:pva-descent}
% =================================================================

The descent from chiral algebras to Poisson vertex algebras (PVAs)
is controlled by six axioms D1--D6.  At genus~$0$:

\begin{enumerate}[label=D\arabic*.]
\item \emph{Leibniz rule}: $\{a_\lambda bc\} = \{a_\lambda b\}c + b\{a_\lambda c\}$.
\item \emph{Sesquilinearity}: $\{\partial a_\lambda b\} = -\lambda\{a_\lambda b\}$.
\item \emph{Skew-symmetry}: $\{b_\lambda a\} = -\{a_{-\lambda-\partial} b\}$.
\item \emph{Jacobi}: $\{a_\lambda\{b_\mu c\}\} - \{b_\mu\{a_\lambda c\}\} = \{\{a_\lambda b\}_{\lambda+\mu} c\}$.
\item \emph{Commutativity}: $ab = ba$.
\item \emph{Unit}: $\{a_\lambda 1\} = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

D2--D5 are proved via geometric arguments: the exchange cylinder
and three-face Stokes identity on $\FM_3(\C)$.  D6 uses the
factorization of $H_3(a)$ and topological contractibility.

The bridge from the $\SCchtop$ operad to PVA axioms operates
in two directions:
\begin{itemize}
\item Operad $\Rightarrow$ axioms (F4): the $\SCchtop$
  structure maps project to PVA operations.  Proved.
\item Axioms $\Rightarrow$ operad (F5, rectification): a PVA
  satisfying D1--D6 lifts to an $\SCchtop$-algebra.  Proved
  via homotopy-Koszulity of $\SCchtop$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Homotopy-Koszulity of $\SCchtop$}
\label{sec:homotopy-koszul}
% =================================================================

\begin{theorem}
The chiral-topological Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$ is
homotopy-Koszul.
\end{theorem}

The proof has three steps:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The classical Swiss-cheese operad is Koszul
  (Livernet, Voronov, Ginzburg--Kapranov).
\item Kontsevich formality gives a quasi-isomorphism
  $\Phi\colon \SCchtop \to \mathrm{SC}$.
\item Transfer: bar-cobar preserves quasi-isomorphisms;
  two-out-of-three gives the result.
\end{enumerate}

The consequence: bar-cobar on $\SCchtop$-algebras is a
Quillen \emph{equivalence}, not merely an adjunction.
Lines (boundary conditions) are unconditionally $\cA^!$-modules.
The dg-shifted Yangian is unconditional.

\section{The modular Swiss-cheese operad}
\label{sec:modular-sc}
% =================================================================

The modular extension replaces trees by stable graphs with
mixed ($\C$-direction and $\R$-direction) edges.  The
relative Feynman transform $F_{\mathrm{rel}}\cO$ sums
over stable graphs with two edge colours:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Closed edges} (genus direction): carry the
  $\hbar$-weight, record non-separating degenerations.
\item \emph{Open edges} (boundary direction): carry the
  boundary insertion data, record separation of marked
  points along~$\R$.
\end{itemize}

The differential contracts one edge; $d^2 = 0$ is the
codimension-$2$ boundary cancellation on the moduli space
of bordered Riemann surfaces.

At genus~$g \geq 1$, the curvature
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$ forces a passage
from ordinary to \emph{curved} Swiss-cheese algebras.
The curvature is the genus-$1$ shadow $\kappa$ from
Volume~I; it measures the failure of the genus-$0$
Swiss-cheese structure to extend uncurved to higher genus.

\section{3D holomorphic-topological QFT}
\label{sec:3d-ht}
% =================================================================

A 3D HT theory on $\C \times \R$ has:
\begin{itemize}
\item Boundary condition on $\{0\} \times \R$: a chiral
  algebra~$\cA$ (the ``boundary VOA'').
\item Bulk: a factorization algebra on $\C \times \R$
  (the $\SCchtop$-algebra from the recognition theorem).
\item Lines: $\cA^!$-modules (boundary conditions for the
  bulk theory restricted to $\R$).
\end{itemize}

The bar-cobar adjunction $B \dashv \Omega$ encodes
boundary data into the bar coalgebra $\barB(\cA)$ and
recovers it via the cobar construction.  On the Koszul
locus, the recovery is exact (Theorem~B from Volume~I).

The holographic modular Koszul datum
$\cH(\cA) = (\cA, \cA^!, \cC, r(z), \Theta_\cA,
\nabla^{\mathrm{hol}})$
packages the complete HT system into a single modular
Maurer--Cartan problem.  The five theorem targets from
Volume~I (A--D, H) are projections of this datum.
